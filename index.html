Introduction

The 1990s were such a defining decade in the rise of the internet as we know it and the personal computing culture. While many companies like Microsoft and AOL have helped shape the technical foundation of the early fragmented web; Apple would influence the way people creatively could connect with it. Throughout the late 1990s, Apple really evolved from a struggling "tech bro" company near a collapse to the huge cultural symbol associated with simplistic artistic expression with a vision with the human-centered vision of technology in mind.

This transformation was not completely driven by hardware, but by a much broader cultural shift in how people would eventually view technology's role in our daily lives. Apple positioned such computers and the internet as a whole; not just as tools for big business or tedious academic work, but rather as gateways to unlimited creativity, communications, and individual personal identity.

Key Quote from Steve Jobs (1998)

"It will set the new gold standard and it is beautiful"
- Steve Jobs, introducing the iMac G3

The Transformation

At the beginning of the 90s, Apple was in a very difficult position. The company was in serious financial turmoil. The company was not competing like they hoped they would. Although the company had a great presence in schools and other creative industries; the mainstream computing world was still dominated by Microsoft and the IBM-compatible PCs.

Research Focus

How Apple's 1990s transformation from near-bankruptcy to cultural icon shaped our modern relationship with technology. From computers as tools to computers as identity. From beige boxes to lifestyle statements. This is the story of how Apple made tech "cool" and changed internet culture forever.
