*** FROM CRISIS TO COOL: THE APPLE TIMELINE ***

1990
October 15:
Apple launches Macintosh Classic, LC, and IIsi - attempting to compete with cheaper PCs
System Software:
System 7 in development - will bring color, virtual memory, and cooperative multitasking
1991
May 13:
System 7 released - first Mac OS with built-in color support
October 21:
PowerBook 100, 140, 170 launched - Apple enters laptop market seriously
Market Position:
Apple holds 8% PC market share, begins slow decline
1992
April-October:
Performa line launches - confused product line begins (over 30 models!)
1993
February 10:
John Sculley coins term "Personal Digital Assistant" (PDA)
August 2:
Newton MessagePad released - Apple's first "Post-PC" device (fails commercially)
June 8:
Michael Spindler replaces Sculley as CEO - crisis deepens
1994
March 14:
Power Macintosh line launches with PowerPC processors - Alliance with IBM/Motorola
Market Reality:
Windows 3.1 dominates. Apple market share falls below 7%
1995
August 24:
Windows 95 launches - Microsoft's cultural moment. Apple seems obsolete.
November:
First Mac clones ship (Power Computing) - desperate licensing attempt
Financial Crisis:
Apple loses $68 million in Q4. Stock hits 10-year low.
1996
February 2:
Gil Amelio becomes CEO - third CEO in 3 years
Q1-Q3:
Apple loses $1 billion. "90 days from bankruptcy" - later revealed by Jobs
December 20:
APPLE BUYS NeXT for $400 million - STEVE JOBS RETURNS AS ADVISOR!
1997 ‚Üê THE TURNING POINT
July 9:
Gil Amelio ousted - Steve Jobs becomes interim CEO (iCEO)
August 6:
Microsoft invests $150 million in Apple - Jobs' master stroke
September 28:
"THINK DIFFERENT" campaign launches - Cultural revolution begins!
November 10:
Apple Store online launches - Direct sales model
1998 ‚Üê THE REVOLUTION
May 6:
iMAC UNVEILED! Bondi Blue translucent design shocks the world
August 15:
iMac goes on sale - 278,000 sold in 6 weeks!
October:
Apple profitable for first time in 3 years - The comeback is real
December:
800,000 iMacs sold - Fastest-selling computer in Apple history
1999
January 5:
Five iMac colors announced - Strawberry, Blueberry, Grape, Tangerine, Lime!
July 21:
iBook announced - "iMac to go" - Clamshell design in Blueberry and Tangerine
September 1:
Power Mac G4 announced - "Supercomputer" for creatives
October 5:
Mac OS 9 released - "The Best Internet Operating System Ever"
Stock Price:
Apple stock up 270% since 1997 - From $4 to $30+ per share
